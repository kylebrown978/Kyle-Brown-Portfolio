MAYFLY — Comet Sample Return

Mission Concept & My Role

MAYFLY is a small-sat concept for returning a regolith sample from the active comet 311P/PANSTARRS. I supported architecture trades and packaged the payload within a compact bus while keeping power, mass, and interface constraints front-and-center. I also built quick scripts to sweep trajectories and margins so the team could compare options rapidly in reviews.

What I owned

  • Payload/bus CAD
  • Propulsion trades (SEP vs. chemical)
  • Trajectory & margin sweeps
  • Interfaces & packaging
  • Review prep

Key contributions

  • CAD & packaging: Modeled the payload instrument bay and mechanical interfaces to the bus; tracked mass properties as the layout evolved.
  • Propulsion trade support: Compared solar-electric vs. chemical stacks, linking power and tankage to timelines and sample-ops windows.
  • Analysis tooling: Wrote scripts to sweep trajectory assumptions and power/mass margins; exported plots directly into design review decks.
  • Integration notes: Flagged thermal and harnessing hot-spots early to inform panel cut-outs and payload connector strategy.
